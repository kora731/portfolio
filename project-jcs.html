Jaunt Cloud Services: Video Product Pipeline

Empower VR Media Creation and Distribution

Overview

Intro

9/2016 - Current With Jaunt Cloud Services(JCS), users can apply cloud rendering to stitch 24 footage together, and transcode them into different formats. The cloud services help prepare video media for distribution on all VR platforms instead of managing files one by one. Along with the rendering, JCS also provides the service modules of Publishing and Analytics to help creators distribute videos and track the audience engagement.

My Role

I work together with 3 PM for different service modules, and collaborate with 4 software engineers on a day to day basis. As the only designer responsible for internal services and tools, I organize regular design critique sessions with the team, and iterate solutions based on the feedback and technical constraints.

Experience Goals

Seamless and easy integration with production flow

Transparent and enable creators to focus on the content

Powerful yet efficient to bring the VR experience to consumers

Design Challenge

Started with experience map, I interviewed internal teams and SME to find out how does the pipeline work, and where are the breakdown points. There are three key problems:

1. Media centric UI rather than project based. Users have a hard time managing media across projects.

2. No transparency on the rendering status, and upload/download sessions are complicated.

3. How to onboard new users to be familiar with the pipeline without training?

Solution Framework

Propose a seamless cloud and local synchronized experience, under project structure.

Takeaways

Design solution needs to balance between future long-term goal and current iterations, how to handle user feedback and understand the insights instead of only following features asked for. To come up with effective solutions, designers need to learn about and prepare for the big picture and understand the why better. For example, a large part of the system today is built upon request, but what turned out happening is that the features built are not used by the end user. When users ask for certain features, should we really follow the comments? or we need to rethink through the deeper reason and flow, why they are like this today. Remind me of Ford’s faster horse theory. Designers need to consolidate the insights, collaboratively define the vision we’re heading towards
