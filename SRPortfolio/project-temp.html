PathFinder: Patient Board for Discharge Planning

Actionable Data Delivered in a Delightful Humane Way

Overview

Intro

PathFinder is a patient board to facilitate discharge planning, presented on the TV while the care team huddles in the morning to help drive collaboration and proactive planning of patient discharge. PathFinder by Qventus is a key offering to customers to streamline patient flow to reduce length of stay and improve flow efficiency.

My Role

I‘m the Lead designer to redesign patient board. From initial brainstorming to final specs, I worked along with the team of PM, engineers to deliver the product within 5 months, which became top sale products and received a lot of positive feedback from customers.

Experience Goals

Business: Reduce LOS to lower cost

User Group: Improve efficiency and proactively address barriers, limited time for each patient, poor UX of EMR system

Product Strategy: stellar visual, scalable configuration

Design Challenge

How to redesign the patient board to enable proactive discharge planning and scalable to other use cases?

Exploration/Research

Use Case: How to support IDR rounding / CN to review patients, like daily standup on the TV and make a decision. so that IP flow and discharge are streamlined and move patients forward in the care process. Reduce length of stay

IP Flow Diagram

What are the critical information to planning discharge process? EDD, Dispo, Barriers.

Solution Framework

Experience Guidelines: Situational Awareness, Progressive Disclosure, Actionable

IxD: EDD Selection, Dispo, Barrier

Outcome

Final product to be popular, gain traction and drives a lot of follow-up sales conversation - currently used in NYP, Emory, PHS, Sutter, Dignity Fairview and more 20+ hospitals. “Finally, I have a technology that makes my job easier.” Charge Nurse https://www.qventus.com/content/uploads/2019/05/Qventus-For-Nursing-Teams-More-Time-For-Patients.pdf With Qventus you have a snapshot of a patient without digging through any charts and without having to ask a lot of questions. You know what the plan is, where they are going, and what's keeping them here.” Registered Nurse at a large health system
