First Experience: User Engagement Journey

How to engage users to get onboard with new features?

Overview

Intro

09/2014 - 03/2015 The first project I lead designing at Autodesk is to deliver an engaging first experience to help users get started with InfraWorks 360, a civil engineering design software with data-rich 3D models. During the customer journey, First Experience covers multiple touch points where users get onboard with the product and develop design skills using InfraWorks 360. Its components consist of product installation & update, first product launch, trial experience and in-product help where the design helps a novice user become an expert.

My Role

I lead design efforts on the first experience project, working closely with Content Developer and product scrum teams. I researched on user onboard experience of civil engineering tools to identify pain points, then designed and built interactive prototype to test with customers. Finally I presented findings in the form of a help pattern library to share across product teams.

Outcome

Result Three design deliverables: Contextual Help Patterns, Product Installation-Tour Design and Seamless Delivery Design

Experience Goals

  • remove software jargon, conversational tone (copy writing)
  • inspiring and ready-to-use contents
  • know and care about who you are: inquiry questions about user background
  • where do users come from (experience map): design for touch points
